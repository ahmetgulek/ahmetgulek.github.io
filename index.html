Postdoc in Economics

Stanford Immigration Policy Lab

I have finished my PhD in Economics at MIT in May, 2025. Starting in September, I will be a Postdoc at Stanford Immigration Policy Lab under the supervision of Jens Heinmueller and Ran Abramitzky. I will enter the academic job market in the 2026-2027 academic cycle.

Research

My research examines two critical challenges facing modern labor markets: the economic impact of unprecedented refugee flows on developing economies and the persistent gender wage gap in developed nations. By combining rigorous causal identification strategies with innovative theoretical and structural frameworks, I answer pressing policy questions and develop novel econometric tools when existing methods fall short. My work has particular relevance today, as global refugee numbers have quadrupled to 45 million in the past decade, while gender earnings inequality remains persistent despite decades of progress in women's labor force participation.
